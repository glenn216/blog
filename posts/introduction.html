Purposive Communication 101: Introduction

A roadmap to effective communication.

Posted by Glenn Alon on May 25, 2023

Purposive Communication teaches us students how to communicate effectively and appropriately in different modes and contexts. This course aims to helps us develop our oral, written, and presentation skills in English for various purposes and audiences. It also teaches us how to use language and non-verbal cues appropriately and persuasively.

Purposive communication is necessary as it allows us to interact with others and appropriately communicate our thoughts, information, points of view, facts, and feelings. It also helps develop our communicative skills and cultural and intercultural awareness. Purposive communication is a skill that can help us achieve our academic or professional goals while also building meaningful relationships.

Some of the activities to look forward to in this course are roleplaying, writing letters, constructing sentences using appropriate grammar and vocabulary, and not to mention, singing a song when someone speaks in a vernacular language!

Roadmap of Effective Communication

Our task performance is part of the roadmap to effective communication because it allows us to practice and improve our skills in a simulated environment based on the given scenarios.

  1. The first task performance is a filmed roleplay. Our group was assigned to roleplay a thesis defense scenario. The topics covered are all the topics during prelims, such as asking/giving information, telling the time, and counting numbers.
  2. The second task performance is a kind of non-verbal communication because we would write a letter which expresses regret and apology.
  3. Lastly, our current task performance in the pre-finals is writing all the learnings we had in the whole semester in this blog.

Purposive Communication 101: Prelims

Tips and Tricks to master your prelims.

Posted by Glenn Alon on May 25, 2023

The prelims term helped me improve my communication skills in basic English. I learned and practiced introducing myself and people in a variety of settings, including formal, informal, casual, and professional. Moreover, I learned how to answer simple YES-NO questions, and how to ask them, and I also learned about the Wh-questions (5W and 1 H).

Not to mention, I learned how to ask and give information about several topics, such as personal details, hobbies, preferences, and many more. In addition, I also learned how to ask for permission politely and appropriately in different contexts.

There are also kinds of topics that I learned relating to numbers, such as telling the time, and the proper sentence using the correct sentence structure when counting things, such as using singular or plural nouns and verbs.

Tips And Tricks

For making introductions, remember to use the right titles and pronouns for varying occasions. Use Mr., Mrs., Ms., or Dr. in formal or professional situations, while in informal or casual ones, we would use first names or nicknames.

For answering and asking YES-NO questions, remember to use the auxiliary verbs do, does, did, can, could, will, would, etc.

Always use the phrases who, what, where, when, why, and how at the beginning of the question while asking Wh-questions (5W and 1H). Furthermore, remember to answer the question with relevant information as well.

For asking and giving information, remember to use the verb to be (am, is, are) for describing yourself and others.

For asking for permission politely and appropriately, remember to use the modal verbs can, could, may, might.

For telling the time and counting things using numbers, remember to use the cardinal numbers while ordinal number when we want to specify the order or position of something in a sequence.

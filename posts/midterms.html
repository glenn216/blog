Purposive Communication 101: Midterms

A skill for life and midterms.

Posted by Glenn Alon on May 25, 2023

During the midterms, I learned a lot of things that helped me express myself better in English. I learned how to talk about my feelings, such as happiness, sadness, anger, or fear. I also learned how to talk about my daily activities, such as studying, working, or playing. Moreover, I learned how to apologize sincerely and politely when I make a mistake or hurt someone. Furthermore, I learned how to accept, and refuse offers or invitations gracefully and respectfully.

In addition, I also learned how to give and ask for directions to someone. I also learned how to describe things and people using adjectives, such as colors, shapes, sizes, etc.

Communication is a Skill for Life

“The way we communicate with others and with ourselves ultimately determines the quality of our lives.”
- Tony Robbins

Communication is a skill that allows us to connect with others in various settings and build varied connections. We can develop and maintain various forms of relationships through speaking effectively, such as friendships, family bonds, romantic partnerships, and professional collaborations.

Furthermore, communication allows us to express ourselves, values, ideas, and emotions. Lastly, communication allows us to communicate and acquire information and knowledge that can improve our learning, decision-making, and problem-solving abilities.
